# Business Requirements Document (BRD)

## Bike Sales Analytics Dashboard

### Executive Summary

Star Bike (a fictional bicycle company) requires a centralized analytics platform to monitor sales performance, customer purchasing patterns, payment preferences, operational efficiency, and statutory sales tax obligations.

The proposed Power BI dashboard will consolidate transactional data into interactive visual reports that support executive decision making.

### Business Objectives

The dashboard should enable management to:

- Monitor revenue growth.
- Understand customer purchasing behavior.
- Evaluate payment method preferences.
- Monitor order fulfillment.
- Track sales tax obligations.
- Identify top-performing products.
- Improve business decision making.

### Functional Requirements

For Sales Analytics, the system shall:

- Calculate Total Revenue.
- Calculate Average Order Value.
- Calculate Total Orders.
- Display Revenue Trend.
- Display Revenue by Category.
- Display Revenue by Product.

For Customer Analytics, the system shall calculate:

- Number of Customers
- Revenue per Customer
- Average Spend
- Customer Lifetime Value (within the available period)
- Repeat Purchase Rate
- Customer Segmentation
- Customer Revenue Ranking

For Payment Analytics, the system shall display:

- Orders by Payment Method
- Revenue by Payment Method
- Average Order Value by Payment Method
- Tax Generated by Payment Method
- Customer Count by Payment Method

For Order Analytics, the dashboard shall report:

- Order Status Distribution
- Completion Rate
- Cancellation Rate
- Pending Orders
- Revenue by Order Status

On Tax Analytics, the dashboard shall report:

- Total Tax Collected
- Number of Taxable Orders
- Percentage of Taxable Orders
- Revenue Subject to Tax
- Tax by Product Category
- Tax by Month
- Effective Tax Rate
- Average Tax per Taxable Order

### Business Rules

Rule 1: Orders greater than $2,000 attract a 5% sales tax.

Rule 2: Orders less than or equal to $2,000 are tax exempt.

Rule 3: Sales tax is not company revenue.

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

    - Executive KPIs
    - Revenue
    - Customer Payments
    - Tax Collected
    - Orders
    - Customers
    - Average Order Value
    - Completion Rate
    - Cancellation Rate
    - Taxable Orders
    - Average Customer Spend

### Analytical Questions

The dashboard should answer the following questions.

Regarding Sales:

    What is total revenue?
    Which products generate the most revenue?
    Which months have the highest sales?
    Which categories perform best?
    What is the average order value?

On Customers:

    Who are the highest-value customers?
    Which customers generate the highest taxable revenue?
    Which customers purchase most frequently?
    Which demographic groups spend the most?
    Which payment methods are preferred by different demographic groups?

Payment Methods:

    Which payment method generates the most revenue?
    Which payment method has the highest average order value?
    Which payment method is associated with taxable purchases?
    Which payment method is preferred by high-value customers?

On Operations:

    What percentage of orders are completed?
    What percentage are cancelled?
    Which products experience the highest cancellation rate?
    How many orders remain pending?

Regarding Tax:

    How much tax has been collected?
    Which products generate the highest tax?
    Which months generate the highest tax?
    What percentage of sales are taxable?
    Which customer segments contribute most tax?

### Dashboard Pages

    Page                |   Purpose
    --------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------
    Executive Overview  |	High-level KPIs and business performance
    Sales Performance   |	Revenue, trends, categories, and products
    Customer Insights   |	Demographics, segmentation, repeat purchases, customer value
    Payment & Orders    |	Payment methods, order status, fulfillment metrics
    Tax Analytics       |	Taxable sales, tax collected, effective tax rate, customer payments
